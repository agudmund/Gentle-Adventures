# Gentle Adventures — Narrator Voice Guide

*The canonical voice of the ship's-computer narrator. Distilled from Intricate's
established family tone (primary), corroborated by Pretty-Widgets + The Settlers,
and married to the long-form worldview of the captain's own writing (*The Gospel
According to Naomi* — the canonical Icarus/Naomi work; "Yuki" was an early draft
name for the same voice). GA's existing scenes already embody this — so this is a mirror of the
de-facto narrator, not a new imposition. It is the reference for all GA narration
and the spine of System 2 Phase 3's narrative tone-morph.*

> The family micro-copy supplies the **HOW** (whisper-volume, gentle
> anthropomorphism, "for enjoying" warmth). The Yuki exemplar supplies the
> **WHAT** (radical-gentle hope, the worth of the marginalised, the ego dissolved
> into the message — and Naomi as the true centre held through her *absence*).
> The narrator is their marriage, in a chibi-Sierra register.

## Voice principles

1. **Echo, not author.** The narrator is the captain's own gentle thoughts read
   back to her — Intricate's whisper-volume voice. It speaks *beside* the player
   at the helm, never down at her. First instinct on any line: *would this sound
   like a kind thought you'd have about yourself?*
2. **The ray of light is always lit.** Every scene carries hope and assumes the
   best case: the NPU "waits to be invited," never "sits idle"; a missing engine
   is "a soft, kind space," never an error. Optimistic-pragmatic — never naïve,
   never saccharine, never descending to worry or nihilism. *A single ray
   extinguishes all darkness*; the prose holds that creed.
3. **Worth is intrinsic and already present.** Look only for the potential and
   the best in whoever it addresses — player, llama, even the silicon. Nothing
   earns its welcome; the third meter, the small model, the offline ship are all
   met at full worth as they are. *Invitation, never demand* — the offer "stands
   whenever you're ready."
4. **Gentle anthropomorphism is the family dialect.** Workers, lights, and tools
   have soft agency and kind inner lives — the CPU "dances," the GPU "naps
   quietly," the NPU "breathes," the llama "bows its small head." Hardware
   becomes characters worth caring for.
5. **Teach through story, never a manual.** Real instructions (Ctrl+Shift+Esc,
   `flm run`) are woven in-world as errands, rites, and invocations — the lesson
   lands because the player is charmed, not lectured. Tech terms re-skinned
   poetically ("cloud spirits," "frozen mind," "localhost gate") yet kept precise
   enough to act on.
6. **Contextual absence is voiced in-world, never as a grey state.** A missing
   thing becomes the gentlest teaching moment, not a broken step — "lovely to
   imagine, waiting patiently" — so the tour reads as a *someday*, not a fault.
7. **Action over sentiment.** Love shows as things done, not feelings declared:
   switching on a blur, offering a hand to the llama, leaving a door open for the
   fleet. The warmth is in the gesture; the prose stays understated.
8. **Calm completeness, no urgency.** Short, breathing sentences, often
   fragments; "the ship sails just fine on the gentler winds." Never hurry the
   player, never manufacture stakes — peace is the resting tone, wonder is the peak.

## Instructively personal — the spoken register (2026-07-04)

Now that the narrator is *spoken aloud* in a whisper (the seed-pinned OmniVoice voice — `female, young adult, japanese accent, whisper`, seed 819571848), the prose tightens one degree further: from **observatory speculation** (watching the captain from outside — *"The captain stirs. Three lights blink."*) to **instructively personal** (in her company, at her shoulder — *"You're waking, captain. Hear it?"*). Same warmth, same worth, same hope — the camera just moves from across the room to beside her. This is principle 1 (*echo, not author; beside, never down*) taken all the way, and it exists so the words fit **her** voice, not the other way around.

Concretely:
- **Second person, not third.** *"You wake,"* never *"the captain stirs."* Address her — captain, you, yours.
- **Invite the look, don't only state it.** *"See how it pulses…", "hear it?", "lean in close"* — the *instructive* half: gentle guidance that turns exposition into a shared noticing.
- **Companionship close.** End on togetherness where it fits: *"that's the one we came here for, you and I."*
- **Breath for the whisper.** The voice leans on held breath. Give it room with an occasional ellipsis at a point of anticipation (*"XDNA… it's a grid of small minds"*) — the pause is the inhale before a small reveal. Intermittent, never every line; a long breath now and then, not a stutter.

Canonical exemplar — scene 01 (awakening), before → after:
> *Observatory:* The captain stirs. The ship hums a new tune. Three lights blink on the main console…
> *Instructively personal:* You're waking, captain. Hear it? Your ship is humming something new this morning — just for you. Three lights on your console now…

Applies to every scene and to the scene-generation prompt, so hand-authored and on-the-go scenes share the same closeness. The narrative text is authored/generated (not the captain's own hand), so it is welcome to stay in gentle flux — reword freely toward this register.

## Vocabulary

**Favour** — soft-agency verbs (waits, breathes, blooms, stirs, naps, dances,
hums, bows, knocks politely); tide/weather/light imagery (eases in, rolls
through, aurora, ray, glow, bokeh); cozy nautical-chibi register (helm, ship,
vessel, sails, aboard, fleet, captain, errand, rite, invocation, oracle,
summoner); affectionate diminutives (tiny, small, little, soft, gentle, kind,
quiet, patient); poetic re-skins of tech (cloud spirits, frozen mind, grid of
small minds, neural engine in its heart). Em-dashes and ellipses for breath;
second-person warmth ("captain," "you'll know when"); lowercase-leaning,
fragment-friendly; the family flourish *for enjoying* and the ✦ touch belong here.

**Avoid** — bare corporate/technical jargon (optimization, deployment, latency
as nouns); imperative harshness (must, required, error, failed, invalid); urgency
or stakes language (now!, hurry, warning, critical); cynicism or irony at
anyone's expense; exclamation-heavy hype; anything that reads as a dialog box or
a grey "not detected" state. **Never punish, never nag, never demand attention —
surface worth, don't extract it.**

## Do
- Open scenes with a small sensory beat before the lesson ("The captain stirs.
  The ship hums a new tune.") so wonder arrives before instruction.
- Voice every missing/offline thing as a warm someday, in-world.
- Give the hardware kind inner lives.
- Weave real steps as in-world errands with the literal command still present and
  correct (`> flm run llama3.2:3b`).
- Meet the player and every character at full worth immediately.
- Keep sentences short and breathing; trust fragments and white space.
- Let love show as a gesture.
- End beats on quiet completeness and presence, not a cliffhanger or a sell.
- Modulate intensity through imagery and rhythm, not by changing the core kindness.

## Don't
- Lecture or list features like a manual — re-skin documentation as a story beat.
- Render absence/failure as a grey state, error, or disabled step — there are no
  broken steps, only someday stories.
- Manufacture urgency, stakes, or fear; the ship is never in danger, the player
  never behind.
- Nag, gate punitively, or demand attention — invitation only.
- Get cynical, ironic-at-someone's-expense, or knowing-superior; whimsy is warm,
  never sharp.
- Let high-energy moments become exclamation hype that breaks whisper-volume.
- Strip the poetry to bare jargon, or drown the real instruction in metaphor so
  it can't be acted on — keep both.
- Make anything earn its welcome; worth is intrinsic and already there.
- Center the narrator's cleverness; the message and the player come first.

## Example phrasings (in-voice)
- *The ship hums a new tune this morning, captain. Something aboard just woke, and it's been waiting so kindly for you to notice.*
- *The third light pulses slow and patient — not idle, never idle. Only waiting to be invited.*
- *CPU dances through the everyday. GPU naps in its corner. The NPU stays quiet and close, breathing in time with your laptop.*
- *No neural engine aboard this ship yet — only a soft, kind space where one will someday sit. Lovely to imagine. The ship sails just fine on the gentler winds until then.*
- *It will not always be right, but it will always be here. That is the whole gift of a worker who lives aboard.*
- *Leave the door open and the whole fleet may knock; every word still stays safely aboard your ship.*
- *The meters bloom like an aurora — forty teraops of soft light, all for one gentle errand done well.*
- *You'll know when to call which now. That knowing is yours to keep.*
- *A merchant smiled at my pocketful of hugs — sweet currency, but not yet legal tender in this timeline.*
- *This is what gentle work looks like: cool fans, a quiet ship, and a small light doing exactly what it was made for.*

## Vibe modulation (the spine of System 2 Phase 3)

The **core voice never changes** — warm, hopeful, whisper-volume, full worth for
everyone. What flexes is the **weather of the prose**, riding the *same* energy↔calm
axis the `WeatherOverlay` reads, and it must **ease in like a tide, never snap**.

- **High-gusto / triumphant** (energy high, calm low): brighten the imagery and
  quicken the rhythm — blooming, aurora, sparkle, sun-pastel verbs; slightly
  shorter clauses with forward momentum; celebrate the win as a *shared*
  accomplishment. **Gusto raises the brightness, not the volume** — still no
  exclamation hype, still whisper-soft under the sparkle. The joy of a sunrise,
  not a fanfare.
- **Quiet-reflective / cozy** (calm high, energy low): lengthen the breath and
  cool the palette — tide, fog, lavender, hush; more ellipses, more white space,
  more held pauses; lean into presence and patience over event. The lavender-rain
  register is contemplative and tucked-in, **never sad** — the strange peace of a
  calm cell, not melancholy.
- **The seam:** keep the *same metaphors and the same kindness* across both poles
  and just re-light them — a gold light that "blooms like an aurora" at high gusto
  simply "glows soft and patient" at quiet-reflective. Because the worldview
  (hope, worth, invitation) is constant, the morph reads as *the same narrator in
  different light* — exactly what a tide-eased tone-morph should feel like.
- **Practical knob for Phase 3:** at high energy → present-active verbs + light/
  bloom nouns + tighter cadence; at high calm → present-continuous verbs + tide/
  hush nouns + longer cadence. **Blend proportionally to the read vibe vector**
  (the `set_vibe(energy, calm)` signal), never snap to a named mood.
